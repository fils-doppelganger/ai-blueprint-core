Lesson 0001: Introduction to the NIAID Blueprint

Purpose, audience, and the five areas—one clear map for your mission.

Learning objective

After this lesson you can name why the Blueprint exists, who it serves, and the five areas you will implement—tied to your MISSION.md.

Why the Blueprint exists

NIAID-funded repositories and data generators need a shared way to describe digital research objects so they are findable, accessible, interoperable, and reusable (FAIR) in the NIAID Data Ecosystem Discovery Portal.

Primary source: NIAID Blueprint for Digital Objects v2 (Markdown).

The five areas (high level)

1. Metadata schema — Minimal schema.org elements (Table 1) for datasets.
2. Persistent identifiers — DOIs, ORCID, ROR, taxon/ontology IDs where required.
3. APIs & machine access — JSON-LD and machine-readable exposure.
4. Citation — How users should cite datasets and related objects.
5. Outreach & training — Points of contact and guidance for communities.

Later lessons unpack each area; reference sheets will live in reference/ as you progress.

Who this is for

  • Data generators — metadata and citation you can supply at deposit time.
  • Repository owners / curators — portal-ready description, PIDs, policies.
  • Developers — APIs, JSON-LD, validation against the spec.
